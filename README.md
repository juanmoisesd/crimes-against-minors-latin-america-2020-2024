## Description

This dataset compiles statistics on crimes committed against minors (ages 0–17) in 19 Latin American countries, covering the period 2020–2024. Data were collected from official sources including the Pan American Health Organization (PAHO/OPS), UNICEF, the International Labour Organization (ILO), the Red por los Derechos de la Infancia en México (REDIM), the Sistema Nacional de Seguridad Pública de México (SNSP), and national statistical institutes.

The dataset includes the following variables: country and geographic sub-region; type of crime (homicide, sexual violence, child labor, injury and abuse); victim age range (0–5, 6–11, 12–14, 15–17 years); year of record (2020–2024); numeric value and unit of measure (total cases, rate per 100,000 inhabitants, estimates); victim's sex (when available); official data source; and methodological notes.

Key findings: Latin America accounts for approximately 50% of global adolescent homicides (ages 10–19), with rates three times higher than other regions. Between 2015 and 2022, 53,318 homicides of minors were recorded in the region (PAHO/UNICEF). Venezuela, Honduras, Colombia, El Salvador, and Brazil show the highest adolescent male homicide rates. Child labor affects more than 10.5 million children in the region, with Guatemala and Bolivia among the most affected countries.
